**Warning: This post contains spoilers for the 2016 sci-fi movie Passengers**

I recently got around to watching [Passengers](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1355644/), and whilst I was aware of a number of less than stellar reviews it was generally pretty enjoyable.  However I was troubled slightly by a plot hole that I haven't seen documented anywhere on the likes of [IMDB](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1355644/trivia?tab=gf&ref_=tt_trv_gf).

![](/assets/img/passengers1.jpg)

The whole issue of the hibernation pods malfunctioning is that there is no way for the protaganists to go back to sleep for the remainder of the trip.  The hibernation pods are designed to keep the passengers asleep, and then wake them up prior to arrival.  Jim (Chris Pratt) reminds Aurora (Jennifer Lawrence) that there was all manner of specialists and equipment involved in putting them under at a facility.  Jim confirms that he checked the manifest and there is no equipment on board that can replicate the task, and even the infirmary does not have the prerequisite equipment.

Here's the thing though - Aurora has booked a round trip on the Avalon.  And according to Gus (Laurence Fishburne) in a deleted scene, the event of the movie take place on the Avalon's sixth interstellar voyage - meaning that it is clearly designed for reuse.  So how are she and the crew planning on going back into cryosleep after the Avalon has spent a year at Homestead II?

One could argue that the necessary skills to perform the procedure are with the sleeping passengers/crew, but what about the equipment? And would those performing the procedure stay behind on Homestead II?

Aurora searches the computer database for information on cryo-sleep, only to find high-level theoretical information for the proprietary technology (is the Homestead Company the only concern embarking on interstellar travel in the future?).  Jim _does_ find that the autodoc in the infirmary can place a patient in a sort of stasis, but only once he uses Gus' security clearance to access the information. Why didn't they try to use this new found access to query the database again on cryo-sleep?

I know it's a minor plot hole, but it has really bugged me! Gah, Hollywood!
